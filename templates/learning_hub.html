The CIA Triad

What is it? The CIA Triad is the foundational model of all cybersecurity policies. It consists of three core principles that every organization must balance to keep their data secure.

Confidentiality

Keeping sensitive data secret and ensuring only authorized people can view it. Achieved through Encryption and strong Passwords.

Integrity

Ensuring data remains accurate, trustworthy, and has not been tampered with by unauthorized users. Achieved through File Hashing and Access Controls.

Availability

Ensuring that systems, networks, and data are actually accessible to authorized users when they need them. Achieved through Cloud Backups and DDoS Protection.

Phishing Attacks

What is it? Cybercriminals use fake emails or messages to trick you into revealing sensitive information like passwords or credit card numbers.

Prevention Tips:
  • Check the sender's actual email address, not just the display name.
  • Look for typos and urgent, threatening language.
  • Never click unexpected links; navigate to the website manually.

Password Security & MFA

What is it? Weak passwords are the #1 cause of breaches. MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication) adds a second layer of security (like an OTP).

Prevention Tips:
  • Use passphrases (e.g., "Blue$piderWalksFast!") instead of short passwords.
  • Never reuse passwords across different sites.
  • Always enable MFA on your email and banking accounts.

Social Engineering

What is it? Manipulating people into giving up confidential info. Attackers might pretend to be IT support, your boss, or a trusted organization.

Prevention Tips:
  • Verify the identity of anyone asking for sensitive data.
  • Be highly skeptical of unexpected phone calls asking for technical access.
  • Remember: Banks will never ask for your PIN or password.

Public Wi-Fi Dangers

What is it? Hackers can easily intercept data transmitted over unsecured public Wi-Fi networks in cafes, airports, or hotels.

Prevention Tips:
  • Use a reputable VPN (Virtual Private Network) to encrypt your traffic.
  • Avoid accessing bank accounts or entering passwords on public networks.
  • Turn off "Auto-connect" on your devices.

Malware & Ransomware

What is it? Malicious software designed to damage your device or steal data. Ransomware locks your files and demands payment to release them.

Prevention Tips:
  • Never download software from unofficial or pirated websites.
  • Keep your operating system and antivirus software up to date.
  • Back up your important files regularly to an external drive or cloud.

Mobile Security

What is it? Smartphones hold our entire digital lives. They are highly vulnerable to loss, theft, and malicious apps hidden in app stores.

Prevention Tips:
  • Use biometric locks (Face ID/Fingerprint) and a strong screen PIN.
  • Only download applications from the official Google Play or Apple App Store.
  • Review app permissions (e.g., does a calculator app need access to your camera?).

Data Privacy

What is it? Oversharing personal information online allows attackers to easily guess your security questions or craft targeted scams.

Prevention Tips:
  • Set your social media profiles to private mode.
  • Never post pictures of your ID, boarding passes, or work badges.
  • Be cautious of online quizzes asking for your mother's maiden name or first pet.

Safe E-Commerce

What is it? Fake online stores and spoofed checkout pages are designed to harvest your credit card information during purchases.

Prevention Tips:
  • Always look for "https://" and the padlock icon before entering payment details.
  • Use a credit card or a service like PayPal instead of a debit card for better fraud protection.
  • If a deal looks too good to be true, it is almost certainly a scam.
